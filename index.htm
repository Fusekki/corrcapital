    HEALTHCARE COST MANAGEMENT SOLUTIONS

    OVERVIEW

    CORR Benefits (“CORR”) and its strategic partners provide three proven healthcare cost containment strategies to both private employers and public organizations. Our solutions are non-disruptive, easily integrated into existing platforms and require no changes to existing vendor relationships.

    Clients, which include Fortune 100, medium and small companies, as well as state and other governments agencies (including self-insured and self-funded organizations), assume no financial risk as compensation is based solely on performance.

    CORR utilizes a three-solution strategy to generate an average of:
    • $2,000 or more per employee per year in savings to the organization; and
    • $3,000 or more per employee per year in additional benefits
    STRATEGY Solution 1: Hospital Billing Audits

    An estimated 7% - 9% of insured employees will require at least one overnight hospital stay each year and, according to the CDC, this hospitalization will represent an excess of 32% of the employer’s annual healthcare costs.

    Through a technology driven medical claims re-pricing and billing review audit by board certified physicians, CORR assists employer sponsored health plans in fulfilling their ERISA defined fiduciary responsibilities by ensuring that only accurate and correctly priced claims are paid.

    90% hospital bills audited by CORR contain overpriced charges and errors. By incorporating an audit process, employers can:

    • REDUCE hospital billing charges by an average of $1,500 per employee per year
    Solution 2: FICA Tax Savings Program

    Under the Affordable Care Act (“ACA”), new incentives are now offered to employers that promote wellness programs and encourage a healthier workforce.

    Through a properly structured ERISA qualified program that is implemented and utilized in compliance with the Internal Revenue Code, HIPAA and ACA, both the employer and employee benefit.

    Employer Benefits
    • REDUCED FICA taxes an average of $600 per employee per year
    • ENHANCED employee benefits
    • IMPROVED employee retention
    • AWARDED an average of $3,000 per employee per year in flex credits to purchase “qualified” benefits under the employer’s §125 plan.
    • Cancer, critical illness, dental, vision, indemnity, AD&D, STD, GAP, Individual Whole Life are all “qualified” benefits as defined by §125(f).
    With 90%+ program enrollment and 98%+ program retention rates CORR delivers maximum and sustainable employer savings. Solution 3: Specialty Drug Savings Program

    According to a 2015 Medical Cost Trend report prepared by PricewaterhouseCoopers, pharmacy costs account for 15% of total healthcare spending and, of this amount, 25% is attributed to utilization of specialty drugs.

    Through a uniquely designed, stand-alone, self-insured medical pharmacy plan that targets a defined list of specialty drugs, employers can:

    • REDUCE specialty pharmacy drug costs by a blended average of $120 per employee per year (on average, $120,000 for every 1,000 employees covered under a group health plan).
    REFERENCES

    CORR Benefits and its strategic partners represent, in aggregate, an excess of 3,000 companies, 25 states, 30 million lives and $15 billion in annual Drug Spend. Upon receipt of an executed non-disclosure agreement, the identity of these strategic partners and client referrals will be made available upon request.
